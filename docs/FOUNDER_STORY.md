# The Story Behind Ortu

I'm **Abhijith** ([abhijithpsubash.com](https://abhijithpsubash.com)) — a software
engineer, and the maker of Ortu.

Ortu didn't start as a product. It started as a small, daily frustration that I
ignored for far too long.

## The problem I lived with every day

As an engineer, my work is a constant rhythm of deploying, building, running, and
debugging — and almost all of it runs on commands I reuse again and again. For
years, my "system" for managing those commands was embarrassingly simple: a single
text file full of them.

The routine went like this. Open the file. Scroll until I found the right command.
Copy it. Switch back to my terminal. Paste. Switch back to the file for the next
one. Over and over, all day long.

It worked — but it quietly broke my flow every single time. Each switch pulled me
out of the moment, and getting back in took longer than the task itself. Multiply
that by a hundred times a day, and you start to feel how much focus is leaking out.

## Why nothing already out there fit

I work across both **Windows and Mac**, switching between them often. The built-in
clipboard tools on both simply couldn't keep up with the way I work — macOS barely
remembers more than the last thing you copied, and Windows' history never felt
built for a developer's hands.

So I looked at the third-party clipboard managers. Some were powerful, but
overloaded with features and buried under a steep learning curve. Others were
clearly abandoned — outdated, unmaintained, and showing their age. None of them
did the one thing I actually wanted: get out of my way and let me keep working.

## So I built the one I wished existed

Ortu is the clipboard manager I always wanted — fast, private, and designed for
people who copy and paste for a living. The whole goal is simple: **let you work
without breaking your flow.**

- **Paste stack** — queue up the commands and snippets you need, then paste them
  one by one across your apps.
- **Instant search** — full-text search finds any clip from your history in a
  single keystroke.
- **Smart auto-grouping** — automatically sorts URLs, code, shell commands, JSON,
  and more.
- **Global-hotkey popup** — summon your clipboard over any app, without ever
  leaving what you're doing.
- **Private by default** — everything stays on your device. No account, no
  telemetry, no cloud. Detected secrets are masked and encrypted at rest.
- **Light and native** — built with Tauri and Rust, so it's a tiny, fast app, not
  a bundled browser.

It runs on **macOS, Windows, and Linux**, and it's **free and open source**.

## What I hope it becomes

The name **Ortu** comes from the Latin *ortus* — "origin," or "a rising." It felt
right: every time you paste, you're bringing something back from your history,
giving it a second life.

I built Ortu to solve my own problem, but I've come to believe a lot of people are
quietly living with the same one — keeping a text file open just to copy from it,
losing their focus one switch at a time. If that sounds like you, Ortu was built
for you. And if it saves you even a fraction of the focus it's given back to me,
then it's done its job.

This is just the beginning. I'd love for you to be part of where it goes next.
